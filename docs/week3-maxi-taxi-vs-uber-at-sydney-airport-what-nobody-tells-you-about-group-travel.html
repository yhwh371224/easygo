Can You Actually Fit Everyone In? The Numbers Nobody Talks About

This is where most group travel planning goes wrong — and it has nothing to do with price.

Uber XL is marketed as a 7-passenger vehicle. In theory, yes. In practice? A group of seven adults with seven checked bags, seven carry-ons, and no luggage trailer has a serious problem. The boot of an Uber XL holds two or three large suitcases at best. The rest ends up on laps, wedged in footwells, or left behind.

The real Uber XL limit isn't 7 people — it's about 4–5 people travelling with normal luggage. Once you add a full set of checked bags, there is simply no physical space for everyone and everything in the same vehicle.

Maxi taxis have more room, but they come with their own hard limit: most maxi taxis max out at around 9 passengers in practice, and — critically — maxi taxis cannot tow a luggage trailer. Unlike private maxi vans, there's no way to add extra luggage capacity to a maxi taxi. What fits in the vehicle is all you get.

So before you even think about price, ask the more important question: can your group and all of its luggage physically fit in one vehicle?

Vehicle Advertised capacity Realistic capacity (with luggage) Luggage trailer?
Standard Uber / Taxi 4 passengers 3–4 with normal luggage No
Uber XL 7 passengers 4–5 with full luggage No
Maxi Taxi Up to 11 passengers Up to 9 with luggage No — cannot tow trailer
EasyGo Maxi Van 8–13 passengers Full group + full luggage Yes — trailer available

For larger groups or anyone travelling with oversized luggage — golf bags, prams, sports equipment, musical instruments — a private maxi van with trailer capacity isn't just more comfortable. It's the only option that actually works.
